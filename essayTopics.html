Essay Topics

  • Why is the Knight first introduced in the general prologue as well as first to tell his tale?
  • When the Knight is first introduced in the General Prologue it is because he is of the highest social class represented in the group.
  • Knights are considered the closest one can get to nobility as they live by an honor code to uphold truth, freedom, and proper morals.
  • When it comes to the telling of the tales the Knight also goes first.
  • Though that decision was made by the drawing of straws the narrator seems to believe that it was not by chance he was the one that went first.
  • For what reasons does the pardoner seem the most offensive?
  • The pardoner's job is to collect money from sinners in exchange for their absolution from their sins.
  • Though this is a common job at the time, and a job supported by the church the pardoner is not a respectable man by any means.
  • The pardoner is a sinner himself as he does his job out of greed not by churchly duty.
  • He admits his greed and is still unrepentant, he is even proud of his sin and that he takes advantage of others in the name of the church.
