Chapter Summaries

  • General Prologue: Introduction
  • The prologue opens with the narrator describing the beginning of spring
  • He explains that at this time of year many people choose to go on a pilgrimage
  • Many of the pilgrims from England choose to go visit holy lands, however an even greater number decide to visit Canterbury and the Canterbury Cathedral where Saint Thomas Becket has left relics
  • The narrator tells of his planned pilgrimage begins in a tavern in Southwark
  • He meets a group of 29 other pilgrims at the tavern looking to travel to Canterbury, and he chooses to tag along
  • The group rests for the night before beginning the journey
  • The narrator interrupts the story to mention that he plans to list all the group members as well as describe them
  • The first character he chooses is the Knight
  • He is very fascinated by the Knight and admires his nobility and politeness
  • The narrator also mentions that the Knight is travelling with his son who's about 20 years old, his soon acts as his apprentice or squire
  • The Squire embodies much of the traits of his father, and fights with great skill and honor
  • However he also is devoted to love and hopes to win the heart of his "lady"
  • His love is very passionate, and he is very skilled in the art of song, the flute, drawing, riding, and writing
  • Despite his strong passion for love and courting a woman he still manages to fulfill all the duties of a son
  • Also travelling with these two is the Knight's Yeoman, or servant
  • He is dressed in a head to toe green outfit that consists of a bow, sword, and shield
  • The narrator then goes on to describe Madame Eglentyne, a Prioress
  • The Prioress, despite not being of royal lineage, tries extremely hard to keep up her appearance in all aspects of life
  • At the dining table she is very prim and proper, she also tries to speak French, however it is with an English accent
  • The prioress is also very compassionate towards animals
  • The narrator describes her of having pretty features and wearing a golden brooch that reads "Love Conquers All"
  • He then shifts to describing The Monk, who surprisingly, despite his title, portrays many surprising qualities for a Monk
  • He is quite handsome and is a passionate hunter, even though it goes against traditions
  • The Friar is the next person on the list
  • He is a very poor, yet extremely jovial man
  • The Friar is a religious man who listens to sinful people for donations, and offers them penance for forgiveness
  • His bright and entertaining attitude makes him a very popular person among the wealthy, who provide him with plenty of money to live happily despite his vow of poverty
  • The next two are the merchant and the clerk, the merchant dresses in nice boots and a fur hat and flaunts wealth, however quite surprisingly and despite all the people that loan him money, he is in debt
  • The clerk is a student of Oxford, he is constantly engrossed in books
  • Following the clerk is the lawyer
  • He is a very wise and busy man, yet he likes to make it appear he is even busier than he actually is
  • General Prologue: Franklin through Pardoner
  • The Franklin is a wealthy farmer who possesses a great deal of land despite not being born of noble lineage
  • He is infatuated with food and his home is filled with meat and drinks
  • He continues on with the 5 Guildsmen who are all artisans
  • All 5 are dressed elaborately in the colors of their guild, and the narrator remarks that all of them could be city officials
  • The narrator addresses the Cook, who is very skilled and well liked, and he then continues on with the Shipman, who is quite the thief and steals alcohol from the merchant who he transports
  • The next description is of the Physician, who bases his practice on astronomy and the four humors
  • He is a very wealthy man and the narrator jokes about him seeming to be only in it for the money
  • He continues on with the Wife of Bath, a slightly deaf seamstress who is quite elaborate in her worship practices
  • This is her third pilgrimage to Jerusalem, and she has also had five husband
  • She has also gone on other pilgrimages to other exotic places
  • On her journey she sits comfortably atop her horse and is quite outgoing giving lots of advice on love
  • A village Parson is described next, a parson is pretty much a pastor
  • The Parson is very compassionate and loyal to his parishioners, he is also very accepting even with sinners
  • With the parson is his brother, a Plowman
  • He is an extremely hard worker, and very loyal to both his friends as well as God
  • The red-haired Miller is quite the character
  • He is extremely strong and a rather gruff man not afraid to show his colors and curse
  • Then their is the Manciple who works as somewhat of a secretary for a group of lawyers, and despite being uneducated seems more intellectual than most of his lawyers
  • The Reeve is an angry man who works managing his Lord's estate, he is quite successful with his work, so much so that he has amassed quite a wealth for himself
  • There is also the Summoner who's role it is to arrest those who violate the laws of the church
  • The Summoner is quite the alcoholic and also an abuser of both his power and of women
  • The Pardoner rides along side the Summoner with flowing blonde hair
  • He is very confident and sings with his friend
  • He has also brought along a number of souvenirs that he collected when visiting Rome
  • The Pardoner's role is to hand out Pardons by the church in exchange for payment, however he abuses his role and is obsessed with money causing the narrator to mock him
  • General Prologue: Conclusion
  • The narrator ends his descriptions of characters and before returning to the story warns the reader that some of the stories may be offensive
  • He then returns to the tavern where the group sits down for a meal provided to them by the tavern owner who is very pleased to have the company
  • The tavern owner devises a plan for the group to enjoy their journey to Canterbury
  • He plans for each member of the pilgrimage to tell two stories on both legs of the journey and the person with the best stories will have dinner provided to him or her upon their return and at the expense of the others
  • The group agrees and decides upon a value of the meal, they then rest for the night before heading off on their journey the next day

  • The Knight's Tale: Part 1
  • His tale begins in Ancient Greece and follows the story of a duke and ruler, Theseus, who rules over the city of Athens
  • While Theseus travels into his city one day on horse he is stopped by four women who kneel in front of him in tears
  • The women inform Theseus that they grieve for their dead husbands who died during the siege of Thebes
  • After the men had died, Creon, the ruler of Thebes, disrespected their bodies and refused them the right of a proper burial
  • Theseus is moved by the women and vows to return to Thebes to claim their dead body
  • When he returns to the battlefield to retrieve the bodies he notices two dying Theban soldiers, however instead of allowing them to die he heals them and then subjects them to a life of prison
  • He then continues on and reclaims the remains of the dead husbands and returns them to their wives
  • The two prisoners go by Arcite and Palamon and are cousins by blood but brothers by declaration
  • The two live in an Athenian tower for years before one spring afternoon when Palamon looks out the tower window and notices Theseus' beautiful sister-in-law Emelye
  • Palamon immediately falls in love and moans with heartache at seeing her, his moans wake Arcite
  • Arcite looks outside also and falls in love as well
  • The two feud over Emelye, however eventually realize their futile attempts as they are both in prison
  • A period of time later a man name Perotheus who is both a friend of Theseus and Arcite petitions to have Arcite freed
  • Theseus agrees yet Arcite must be banished from Athens
  • Arcite is jealous of Palamon as he can still see Emelye each day, while Palamon is jealous of Arcite as he is free and could perhaps sneak back into Athens and take Emelye by force
  • Part 2
  • A while later, while asleep Arcite is visited in his dreams by Mercury, the messenger god
  • Mercury tells him he must return to Athens
  • Arcite, though frail with lovesickness, resolves to disguise himself and return
  • He receives a job as a page for Emelye's chamber and changes his name to Philostrate
  • Though Arcite is close to Emelye he is not as close as he wants to be leaving him conflicted on whether to stay or not
  • Almost simultaneously Palamon escapes from prison after 7 years
  • While Palamon is running through nearby woods he hears Arcite singing and the two end up running into each other
  • The two argue again about Emelye and the vow to duel the next day for the right to her
  • Their dual is very brutal as the two club each other without cease until Theseus comes across them
  • After being stopped the two reveal their real identities and admit their love for Emelye
  • Theseus wants to kill them on the spot, however he consults with his court who chose to show them mercy
  • The court then decides to hold a tournament almost a year from the day in which the two men are pitted against each other each receiving a hundred other men in order to help their cause
  • The winner of the tournament will win the right to marry Emelye
  • Part 3
  • In the time leading up to the tournament Theseus erects a giant stadium for it to be held as well as three temples, one for Venus (the love goddess), one for Mars (The war god), and one for Diana (the chastity goddess)
  • The two men assemble powerful armies and prepare for the tournament
  • In the days before the tournament Palamon visits Venus' temple and prays to her asking to help him win Emelye's hand in the name of love, he ends up receiving a sign which he takes to be in the positive
  • The next morning Emelye visits the temple for Diana and prays to have the marriage stopped as she desires to remain a virgin
  • However she learns that remaining a virgin is not in her cards and that she must marry one of them
  • The day before the tournament Arcite makes his visit to Mars' temple and prays for victory on the battlefield
  • He also receives a positive sign
  • The gods scheme above about the tournament, Saturn, Venus' father tells her that despite the positive sing for Arcite, Palamon will win Emelye's hand in the end
  • Part 4
  • The battle finally is set to begin, and both the armies look impressive
  • However after a hard fought battle Arcite takes his opportunity to disarm Palamon and declare his victory
  • Emelye is overjoyed that the battle is over and her husband has been decided, however much as the gods predicted, and actually controlled, Arcite is crushed by his horse while riding out of battle and nears death
  • While on his deathbed he expresses that Emelye should consider Palamon for marriage
  • Everyone in Athens are very sad by Arcite's death and mourn for many months
  • However the two most affected are Emelye and Palamon who mourn for many years until one day Theseus demands they get over it
  • The two decide to marry and enjoy a long and happy marriage

  • She declares that she is an expert on marriage as she alone has been married 5 times, the first at the age of twelve
  • She believes that it is God's plan for her to be married so many times and she argues that God made us to procreate so he obviously would not be against this
  • She adds that though virginity is often preached, without sex no virgins could be born
  • The Pardoner interrupts the Wife and asks a question about his upcoming marriage, however he is told to listen to the story for it should have his answer
  • The Wife explains that three of her husbands were very good to her, while the other two were the opposite
  • She also adds that she was a very demanding wife and often manipulated them to receive money and gifts
  • Her husbands were all at her command

  • She then gets sidetracked and talks about how beautiful she once was and the fact that she has lost most of it with age

  • The tale begins with the Wife telling of how back in the day of King Arthur, Britain was inhabited by fairies and elves, however in recent times they have been replaced by friars and mendicants
  • The friars are malicious men and she claims they rape women, and even though in the day of the incubi and fairies the incubi raped women, those days were much better because the incubi were respectful and at least got the women pregnant
  • One day a Knight from King Arthur's court was so lustful that when he came across a beautiful young woman one day he decided to rape her
  • The court is outraged over the scandal, and many believe he should be put to death
  • However some of the women on the court want to give him a second chance
  • It is decided that he will have a chance to prove himself worthy by discovering the thing that women want most in the world and return to the court with his findings
  • He has a year for the quest and if he fails he will be beheaded
  • He begins his journey wandering the countryside and looking for an answer, however every women he encounters seems to provide him with a different one
  • He seems to have run out of options until one day as the deadline approaches he sees a group of women dancing, however when he approaches the group it vanishes and one older women remains
  • Desperate he asks for herself and she surprisingly says that she can help him on one condition. He must give himself to her upon being pardoned
  • When the two arrive at the court he tells the court that he has learned that all women want to be in charge of their husbands and lovers more than anything
  • Sure enough, this is the right answer and his life is spared, however as promised he must now marry the old, ugly hag who provided him with the answer

  • When he explains that he is upset to have married such an ugly and poor woman, she gives a response that is quite surprising
  • She asks him whether he would rather be a poor man of noble and strong character, or a rich man who is shameful and villainous
  • She gives him an opportunity to answer a question, whether he would have an ugly but loyal wife, or a young but unfaithful wife
  • He responds by saying that she should choose what she thinks best for him
  • Because the woman has now had what she desired most, full control over her husband, she is very pleased and transforms into a woman that is both beautiful and faithful

  • The Tale of the Nun's Priest
  • The story begins by introducing an elderly widow who lives a humble life with her two daughters
  • The widow has very few possessions, a few sows, cows, a sheep, and a few chickens
  • One chicken has been given the name Chanticleer ("sings clearly" in French)
  • He is a very impressive rooster and has the most magnificent and attractive coat
  • Due to his looks and an impressive attitude every other female chicken, or hens, are attracted to him
  • However their is one in particular that he is also very fond of, a female named Pertelote
  • The two are very fond of each other and have a great relationship
  • One evening while Chanticleer is asleep with all the other hens he has a dream of a beast coming and taking him away
  • When he awakes he tells Pertelote and says he is very worried, however she claims their is nothing to worry about as it is just a dream
  • Chanticleer insists that it was more than that, and sites many examples of others who had premonitions in their dreams that came true
  • A while goes by and nothing seems to happen to fulfill his dream and Chanticleer begins to forget completely about it, however one day he has a sudden wave of sadness and begins to think about the premonition
  • Sure enough that day a fox approaches the farm and comes across an alone Chanticleer

  • Chanticleer gladly obliges and raises his neck to sing, however the fox takes the opportunity to grab him by the neck and drag him away
  • Meanwhile Pertelote notices Chanticleer is missing and is horrified
  • She runs into the barn and lights herself on fire because she is so upset about not believing him
  • A huge commotion erupts in the barn and even the fox who hasn't traveled very far can hear it
  • Chanticleer who is still alive in the fox's mouth convinces him to turn around and see what he has caused
  • When the fox turns he loses just enough grip on Chanticleer allowing the chicken to escape up a tree
  • The fox, furious, tries to woo him again and coax him down the tree yet Chanticleer refuses
  • The moral of the story is to never trust a flatterer
  • The novel ends with this story and the host of the group commenting that he wishes the Priest good luck
